The Myanmar junta has brought more charges including high treason and unlawful association against Ko Wai Moe Naing, a detained anti-regime figure who led protests in Sagaing Region’s Monywa town, bringing to 10 the number of cases filed against the 26-year-old Muslim pro-democracy activist. Ko Wai Moe Naing, one of the most prominent faces of Myanmar’s protest movement against the junta, was violently abducted on April 15 as hewas leading a motorbike rally.
